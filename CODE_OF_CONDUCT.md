# Code of Conduct

Scrollr is an open community. We expect everyone — maintainers,
contributors, and users — to treat each other with respect.

## In short

- Be kind.
- Be patient.
- Assume good intent, but speak up when something feels off.
- Critique ideas, not people.
- Disagree politely; accept decisions you disagree with gracefully.

## What is not welcome

Personal attacks, demeaning comments, sustained disruption of
discussions, or any conduct that a reasonable person would consider
harassment. If you are not sure whether something crosses the line,
it probably does — ask a maintainer before posting it.

## Scope

This applies in every Scrollr-related space — GitHub issues, pull
requests, discussions, Discord, email, and any public or private
communication about the project.

## Reporting

If someone violates this code, report it privately to
[conduct@myscrollr.com](mailto:conduct@myscrollr.com). Reports are
confidential. Maintainers will respond within three business days.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may, at their discretion, remove comments, close issues,
revoke contribution access, or ban contributors for conduct that
violates this policy. Decisions are final; appeals may be sent to the
same address.
